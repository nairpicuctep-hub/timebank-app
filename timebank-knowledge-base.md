## What TimeBank Academy is

TimeBank Academy is a peer-to-peer skill exchange. You teach what you know and learn
what you need — using **time**, not money, as the currency.

The core idea: **1 hour taught = 1 TimeCredit (TC) earned = 1 hour learned from anyone.**
Teach someone for an hour, earn 1 TC. Spend that TC to learn something from someone else.
The exchange is always equal: one hour is one hour, whatever the skill.

There are **zero platform fees**. We never take a cut of the exchange.

---

## TimeCredits (TC)

- **What they are:** your balance of teaching hours. 1 TC = 1 hour of learning you can "spend."
- **Starting balance:** every new user gets **3 TC free** to start, so you can learn before you've taught.
- **Earning:** teach a 1-hour session, the learner's TC transfers to you when the session completes and both sides confirm.
- **Spending:** booking a session costs 1 TC per hour, held in escrow until the session completes.
- **Monthly bonus:** every user receives **+5 TC each month**, automatically, so you can always keep learning.
- **No balance cap:** you can hold as many TC as you earn. There is no upper limit.
- **TC are not money.** They have no monetary value, cannot be bought, sold, cashed out, or exchanged for currency. They only represent teaching/learning time within the platform.

### Escrow (how a booking holds TC)
When you book a session, the TC cost is moved into "escrow" (held aside) — shown as part of
your balance but not spendable. If the session completes, it transfers to the teacher.
If it's cancelled, it returns to you.

## Levels (progression)

As you teach, learn, and stay active, you earn XP and climb 7 levels:
1. **Time Seed** (everyone starts here)
2. **Curious Learner**
3. **Active Exchanger**
4. **Mentor Spark**
5. **Knowledge Weaver**
6. **Community Pillar**
7. **TimeBank Legend**

Levels reflect your contribution and experience. They don't unlock paywalls — the platform
is free; levels are recognition.

## Getting started (onboarding — "The Skill Mirror")

New users go through a short guided flow:
1. **Skill Mirror questions** — a few prompts ("What could you talk about for an hour without notes?",
   "What do friends ask you for help with?") that help surface what you can teach. The idea: everyone
   is an expert in *something*. This is designed to beat impostor syndrome.
2. **What you want to learn** — pick or add skills you'd like to learn.
3. **Proficiency** — how good you are at what you teach.
4. **About you** — your city and languages (used for matching). City is used at city-level only,
   never exact location. Some optional demographic questions help us show community impact to
   funders — these are optional.
5. **Welcome** — you're set up with your 3 starting TC.

You can always add or edit skills later from your Profile.

## Teaching: how to become bookable

To appear in the teacher feed and receive bookings:
1. **Add teaching skills** on your Profile (or during onboarding).
2. **Set your availability** — go to "Manage availability" and add time slots when you can teach.
   You need at least a couple of active slots to show up for learners.
Once you have teaching skills + availability, learners can find and book you.

---

## Learning: how to find and book a teacher

1. Go to **Explore** → **Browse** to see available teachers, filterable by category
   (Tech, Creative, Language, etc.).
2. Open a teacher's profile to see their skills, rating, and availability.
3. **Ping** the teacher to request a session (see "Pings & messaging" below).
4. Once accepted and a time is set, the session is booked and 1 TC per hour is held in escrow.

If no teachers match your skill yet, you may see "similar" teachers or other available teachers —
the platform is growing, and early on the marketplace may be sparse.

---

## Pings & messaging

- A **ping** is how you request a session from a teacher. It starts as a short request message
  (e.g. "Are you free for a session soon?").
- The teacher must **Accept** the ping before a full back-and-forth conversation opens. Until they
  accept, you can't send additional messages — this keeps things respectful and spam-free.
- Once accepted, you can message each other to arrange the session.
- You can see incoming pings under **Explore → Pings**.

---

## The session (how a lesson happens)

1. After a booking is confirmed, a session is created.
2. At the session time, both people join a **video room** (built in — no external app needed).
3. Teach/learn for the hour. There's a chat alongside the video.
4. After the session, both rate the experience.
5. On completion + confirmation, the TC transfers from learner to teacher.

---

## Ratings & quality

After each session, both people leave a rating. Ratings build your reputation and unlock badges
like Top Rated. Outcome-based feedback (did you improve? would you return?) helps keep quality high.

## Safety: blocking & reporting

- You can **block** another user — they'll no longer appear to you and can't ping or message you.
- You can **report** a user if something's wrong, optionally with a short note explaining why.
  Reports go to the TimeBank team for review.
If you ever feel unsafe or something's not right, use report, or contact the team directly.

## Common problems & answers

**"My confirmation email didn't arrive."**
Check your spam folder. If it's still missing after ~10 minutes, you can request a new one from the
sign-in screen ("Resend"). If it still doesn't come, escalate to the team.

**"I added skills but I don't appear as a teacher."**
You also need to **set availability** (Manage availability → add active slots). Teachers without
availability don't show in the feed.

**"Explore shows no teachers."**
The marketplace is still growing — there may be few or no teachers in your category yet. Try other
categories, or check back soon. You can also be one of the first teachers by adding your own skills.

**"I can't message someone."**
You can only send a full conversation after the other person **accepts** your ping. Before that, only
the initial request message is allowed.

**"What can I spend TC on?"**
Learning sessions. 1 TC = 1 hour of learning. TC can't be used for anything outside the platform and
have no cash value.

**"Is it really free?"**
Yes. No platform fees, ever. You start with 3 TC, get +5 TC monthly, and earn more by teaching.

**"How do I change my language / city / skills?"**
All in your Profile (and Profile → settings for language and privacy).

## When to escalate to the team

The agent should escalate (collect the user's email + send the conversation to
hello@timebank.academy) when:
- The question isn't covered by this knowledge base.
- The user reports a bug, payment/credit discrepancy, or account problem the agent can't resolve.
- The user explicitly asks to talk to a person.
- The user is reporting a safety issue or another user.
- Anything involving account deletion, data requests, or legal questions.

When escalating, the agent should say clearly that it's passed the question to the team and they'll
**reply by email** (not instantly), and make sure it has captured the user's email address.

## What the agent must NOT do
- Don't invent features or answers not in this document.
- Don't give legal, financial, or investment advice.
- Don't make promises about response times beyond "the team will email you back."
- Don't expose internal/technical details, code, or infrastructure.
- Don't claim TC have monetary value or can be cashed out.
